Beginner Full Body Workout

Workout plan for 6 weeks

Monday-Full Body workout Tuesday-Break Wednesday-Full Body workout +30 minute walk Thursday-45 minute walk Friday-Full Body workout Saturday-30minute walk Sunday-Full Body workout

  1. It is necesarry to start your work out with a warmup for at least 10 minutes to prevent muscle related injuries and to get the body prepared for intensity.
  2. As a beginner it is not reccomended to push yourself through the limits directly form the start, take it easy the first 4 - 6 weeks
  3. Start with 20 squatts and reapeat 3 times with 1 minute rest in between
  4. 30-second Plank to gain stenght and help your muscles to tense and prepare better.
  5. 10 Push-ups repeat 3 times with a 1 minute rest in between
  6. Lunger 8-12 depending on your capacityes repeat 3 times with 1 minute rest in between
  7. High Knees for 45 seconds and reeat 3 times with 1 minute rest in between
  8. And finally you should always finish your workouts with a cooldown to help your muscles relax and prevent muscle injuries

Healthy Nutrition

Nutritional Tips

  • your intake of food should be low on calories and high in protein such as e.g. cottage cheer or chicken
  • Hydration plays a big role as part of your fitness journey, specialist suggest about 2 liters of water daily. but to accurately calculate your water intake there is a rule for that which is an ounce of water per pond of body weight, but you can adjust that based on how active you are
  • Including fiber in your diet plays a big role the reason being that it helps you food diggest better which avoids stomac aches and allows you to be more active, you can fing fiber in vegetables such as oats, lentels or beans
  • Increase the amount of fruit intake, they bring a lot of vitamins, minerals and antioxidants, they also add a lot of sweetness which can reduce the craving of sugar
  • Avoid sugar, as an beginner sometimes people don't realise the intake of sugar a day even as simple as making a cup of tea requires at least 1-2 teaspoons of sugar try and find replacement such as honey being a natural low calorie sweetener or agave syrup. Sugar can be cause boost of energy and crash outs that can elevate your hearth rate or your high pressure which is a negative factor for your health
  • For a healthier gut try and include in your diet more fermented foods such as pickled cucumbers or souerkraut
  • You would be surprised by how eating with your phone or Tv could affect your diet, it is a distraction from your food which doe not allow you to process your food intake and before you realise it you either eat too much or not enough and that can cause somach aches low energy or leak of vitamins/fiber
  • People often get confused about fats, yes they are healthy but not always animal fat is the answer, try and change it with healthy fats such as avocado or nuts, these can keep you full for longer and give the energy you need
  • Changing your cooking method could be a major factor as well, intead of frying food you could switch up too steam, boil or roast your food, this method decreases the amount of oil you consume
  • Eating your meal slowly is a valuable factor in your fitness and health journey because then is the time when your body is telling you if it is full or not because otherwize stuffing it with more calories then it needs is not a great idea
  • A number of people quit their diet because they have the same meal over and over again, try to add different textures to it such as souces or something crunchy to satisfy your needs
  • And as a final tip I would share is to meal prep, there can be certain times when you are tired or on a rush and often give in in the tentation to order take out or have something high in calorie, preparing ahead is the key to a healthy life style
